Sleep is one of the most researched areas of human health and wellness.

The Recovery Elite guide provides educational information about sleep optimization strategies based on scientific literature.

This digital resource explores sleep architecture, circadian rhythms, and practical approaches to creating better sleep conditions.

What this educational guide covers:

Understanding Sleep Architecture

Learn about the four stages of sleep—light sleep (N1 & N2), deep sleep (N3), and REM sleep—and how these stages cycle throughout the night in approximately 90-minute intervals.

The Three Pillars Concept

The guide discusses three areas of focus: restorative sleep practices, stress hormone management, and understanding inflammation's role in sleep quality.

Sleep Environment Factors

Detailed information on optimizing bedroom conditions including darkness, temperature (research suggests 60-67°F/15.5-19.5°C), noise control, bedding quality, and air quality.

Circadian Rhythm Education

Understanding your body's natural 24-hour biological clock, how light exposure affects it, and strategies for maintaining consistent sleep-wake schedules.

Pre-Sleep Practices

Information about 2-hour wind-down routines, the 4-7-8 breathing technique, blue light management, and evening nutrition considerations.

Natural Sleep Support Information

Educational content about melatonin, magnesium, valerian root, and L-theanine, including what research says about typical dosages and timing. This is informational only—not recommendations.

Exercise and Sleep Timing

How physical activity timing relates to sleep quality, with information on aerobic exercise, resistance training, and mind-body practices like yoga.

30-Day Learning Framework

A structured approach to implementing sleep hygiene practices progressively over four weeks, with weekly focus areas.
